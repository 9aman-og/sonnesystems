Research focus

Four hard questions. Clear experiments.

We work on a small set of connected problems where careful controls matter more than impressive language.

Can spiking networks help in practice?

We compare spiking and conventional neural networks under matched budgets to find where sparse event-based computing truly helps.

When should a model change?

We test how continual systems can reuse what they know, adapt locally, or grow only when fresh evidence justifies it.

What can a model guarantee?

We study robustness and invariance with proofs, experiments, and explicit limits on what a certificate can mean.

How much compute is enough?

We examine early exit, sparse activity, and hardware-aware cost so efficiency claims survive realistic accounting.
