Research

Claims should earn their confidence.

We study advanced AI through controlled experiments, matched comparisons, and written limits. The goal is not to make every idea look successful. It is to learn which ideas remain useful after a fair test.

Spiking intelligence

When do spiking-convolutional hybrids help?

Spiking neural networks can process sparse events, but that does not automatically make them more accurate or efficient.

Our controlled study tests a content-adaptive spiking neuron called CA-LIF. Several apparent wins disappeared under matched controls. A smaller result remained: at low timestep counts, the hybrid used less estimated energy than matched dense networks on grayscale Fashion-MNIST and MNIST tasks.

Known limit

The advantage vanished on RGB CIFAR-10, where input encoding dominated the cost. This is a bounded result, not a general efficiency claim.

Continual learning

When should a model reuse, repair, or grow?

A continual system should not add a new expert for every surprise. It should first ask whether what it already knows can explain the new situation.

Project SOL develops evidence gates for structural change. Candidate mechanisms are compared against the full set of reusable alternatives on fresh data before they can enter the model library. Related work studies predictive states, local adapters, and confirmation-gated growth.

Known limit

The results support a research protocol for controlled adaptation. They do not establish general-agent superiority, architectural novelty, or measured neuromorphic efficiency.

Robustness

Can a guarantee survive a change in lighting?

A standard robustness certificate can become useless when image contrast changes, even when a person sees the same object.

Our construction combines a rank-based image front end with a Lipschitz network. It gives exact invariance to monotonic lighting changes and keeps the certificate valid in rank space.

Fundamental limit

On images with large flat regions, exact illumination invariance and a useful pixel-space certificate cannot both be guaranteed. The paper proves this boundary rather than hiding it.

Technical report

SpikingMammal: a hybrid classifier with early exit.

This engineering study combines a conventional visual backbone, an out-of-distribution gate, and a spiking classification head.

The system reached 83% accuracy across twelve cat breeds, rejected dogs in the test set with 97.2% accuracy, and used an average of 12.1 out of 20 available timesteps.

Known limit

This is an integration of known techniques, not new science. The sparse operation count does not prove a real energy advantage on standard CPU or GPU hardware.

Research standard

How we decide what to claim.

Freeze the question

Define the comparison and decision rule before reading the result.

Match the control

Compare budgets, parameters, operating points, and seeds fairly.

Keep negative results

Retracted wins and failed gates remain part of the research record.

State the boundary

Separate what the evidence shows from what remains an open question.
